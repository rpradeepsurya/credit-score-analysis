Credit Score Analysis

By Pradeepsurya Rajendran | May 07 2023

Problem Statement

Global finance companies usually uses basic banking and credit-related information to determine the credit worthiness (also known as credit score brackets) of customers. Lenders uses these score brackets to categorize borrowers into different risk levels and also to assess their likelihood of default. Credit scores depend on wide variety of factors such as annual income, credit utilization ratio, number of bank accounts, number of credit cards, etc. Calculating and classifying customers into different score brackets are complicated and challenging for financial companies to do manually. Inaccurate credit score classification can lead to potential financial loss. Therefore, there exists a significant requirement for accurate and efficient credit score brackets classification models that can assist in making informed decision. Thus, it is a significant problem in the financial and lending industries. In this project, the objective is to analyse the dataset with banking and credit related information to identify key features that impact credit score brackets, evaluate different machine learning algorithms to come up with a model that can accurately classify the scores without bias. Thus, the contribution of this project is crucial in the lending industry, as the development of accurate credit score classification models can help promote equality, fairness, and efficient lending practices.

Data Source

The dataset is available in Kaggle and is open-sourced and structured. It contains basic banking details and information pertinent to credit for multiple customers as the input features. All these data were categorized into different credit score brackets (Poor, Good and Standard) and these score values belong to the target variable. There are a total of hundred thousand records, with twenty seven input features and one target variable.
